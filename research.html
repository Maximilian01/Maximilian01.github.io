Thesis Work

I am currently working on a thesis which combines Model-Based Software Engineering, Bluetooth Low Energy, and embedded systems.

My thesis's abstract is:
Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) is a wireless communication platform designed for the age of mobile devices. It is flexible, efficient, and consumes very little power. As a result, it has become one of the most widely supported wireless technologies within the embedded systems ecosystem. Unfortunately, BLE's flexibility and widespread adoption has resulted in the standard being implemented in many significantly different ways. This makes developing embedded BLE applications for a wide range of devices difficult. In addition, susceptibility to errors and wildly different hardware makeups further contribute to the challenge of developing embedded BLE applications. MoBLE aims to alleviate these concerns by leveraging the power of Model-Driven Software Engineering (MDSE). MDSE utilizes modeling and abstraction to make complex software easier to write. MoBLE will be a Domain-Specific Modeling Language (DSML) that will be used to abstractly represent BLE applications. Such a DSML would allow developers to create embedded BLE applications without requiring the embedded system-specific knowledge necessary to actually implement them.
I expect to complete this thesis by May 2024.
